Tourist Places

City Palace

The City Palace of Udaipur, built in 1559 by Maharana Udai Singh II, is a stunning blend of Rajput and Mughal architecture overlooking Lake Pichola. This grand palace features ornate balconies, courtyards, and breathtaking lake views. Key attractions include the Sheesh Mahal, Mor Chowk, and the Crystal Gallery showcasing rare artifacts.

Lake Pichola

Lake Pichola, built in 1362, is a breathtaking artificial lake in Udaipur, surrounded by grand palaces, temples, and scenic ghats. It offers stunning views of the City Palace, Jag Mandir, and the Taj Lake Palace. Visitors can enjoy serene boat rides, especially at sunset, for a magical experience. The lake reflects Udaipur’s royal charm and is a perfect spot for photography. Open for boat rides from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, it remains one of the city's most iconic attractions.
Bahubali Hills

Baahubali, a legendary warrior from Indian mythology, symbolizes strength, valor, and righteousness. His story, rooted in Jain texts, tells of his renunciation of power after a great battle with his brother, Bharata, leading to his spiritual awakening.His name remains a symbol of courage, sacrifice, and wisdom in Indian history and culture.

Fateh Sagar

Fateh Sagar Lake, built in 1687 by Maharana Jai Singh and later renovated by Maharana Fateh Singh, is a beautiful artificial lake in Udaipur. Surrounded by the Aravalli Hills, it features three scenic islands, including Nehru Park, a popular tourist attraction. Visitors can enjoy boating, camel rides, and lakeside cafes while admiring the stunning sunset views. The lake is a perfect spot for relaxation, photography, and experiencing Udaipur’s serene charm.
Moti Magri

Moti Magri, or Pearl Hill, is a historic hilltop in Udaipur, offering stunning views of Fateh Sagar Lake. It is famous for the equestrian statue of Maharana Pratap, honoring the legendary Rajput warrior and his loyal horse, Chetak. The site also features a museum showcasing Rajput history, scenic gardens, and a light and sound show narrating Mewar’s valor.

Saheli Ki Bari

Saheliyon Ki Bari, or the Garden of Maidens, is a beautiful historic garden in Udaipur, built by Maharana Sangram Singh II in the 18th century. Designed for the royal women, it features lush lawns, marble pavilions, lotus pools, and ornate fountains. The garden’s rain fountains were specially designed to recreate the feel of monsoon showers all year round.Open from 9:00 AM to 7:00 PM, it is a must-visit for those seeking tranquility and architectural beauty.
